1) Logic Operators:
A logical operator treats operands as being true or false, and evaluates to true or
false. Logical operators include and, or, not.

2) Logic Table:
Table of Contents. truth table, in logic, chart that shows the truth-value of one or 
more compound propositions for every possible combination of truth-values of the propositions making 
up the compound ones. It can be used to test the validity of arguments.

3) Precedence Rules:
The order in which operators are evaluated in an expression are known as
precedence rules.

4) Decision Expressions:

Decision making structures require that the programmer specifies one or more 
conditions to be evaluated or tested by the program, along with a statement or 
statements to be executed if the condition is determined to be true, and optionally, 
other statements to be executed if the condition is determined to be false.
